# Accessible Reading Pack

> Turn long, dense, or visually noisy digital content into something you can actually read, hear, and navigate.

## What this is for

This workflow is useful for:

- people with dyslexia, low vision, fatigue, or attention overload
- anyone dealing with dense PDFs, long webpages, or messy screenshots
- students and knowledge workers who need a lower-friction reading setup

The goal is not one app. The goal is a repeatable reading mode for the content in front of you.

## Workflow

### 1. Pick the right reading surface

Use the reading surface that matches the source:

- webpage: Edge Immersive Reader or Android Reading mode
- selected text in an app: Speak Selection or Select to Speak
- screenshot or photo: Live Text first
- full screen reading on iPhone: Spoken Content (Speak Screen / Speak Selection)

### 2. Build your default quick path

On iPhone:

1. Go to Settings > Accessibility > Spoken Content.
2. Turn on `Speak Selection` (highlights and reads selected text) or `Speak Screen` (swipe down with two fingers to read the whole screen).
3. Use `Live Text` for screenshots, scans, or photos with text.

On Android:

1. Install and enable `Reading mode`.
2. Turn on its accessibility shortcut.
3. Turn on `Select to Speak` for apps or views where you only need part of the screen read aloud.

On Windows:

1. Open long webpages in Microsoft Edge.
2. Use `Immersive Reader`.
3. Adjust spacing, font, theme, and `Read Aloud`.

### 3. Save the content in the most readable form

If the source is bad:

1. extract text from the image or PDF
2. paste it into a cleaner note or doc
3. let your device read that clean version aloud

This usually beats forcing yourself through a badly formatted original.

### 4. Create a fallback for reading-heavy weeks

Create a note called `Reading fallback`.

Keep:

- your preferred reading voice and speed
- a short prompt for AI summaries
- the apps you trust for reading aloud
- your default export target for cleaner notes

## Failure modes

- Reading tools help access. They do not magically fix bad source material.
- OCR from photos and scans still needs a quick spot check.
- Some Android reading tools do not support every app or content type.
- AI summaries are useful as support, not as the only source for study or compliance reading.
